FRC COMPUTER VISION

As captain of my school's FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) team until graduation, I organized meetings, presentations, led robot programming, and led business activities for our team. We build a ~100-lb robot each year.

For the past two years in FRC, I’ve worked on vision tracking (computer vision, using cameras) for our team’s robots. My programs, running on embedded systems such as the Jetson TX1 and Raspberry Pi, use video cameras and lights to detect field targets in real-time.

My most recent FRC Computer Vision project used Azure Machine Learning. I submitted it to the BOS Raspberry Pi Contest, where I came in first place and won $2,500 in sponsorships for our team.

The technologies I’ve used in these projects include Microsoft's CustomVision.AI, OpenCV, Python, and more.
